# About — Smith Lakes Property Review

## About Smith Lakes Property Review

Smith Lakes Property Review is an editorial desk publishing ranked lists, market guides, and field notes on waterfront real estate across the American Southeast. We work in a niche where generic real estate coverage tends to under-perform — and we treat it accordingly: small lake communities, dock-permit nuance, agent-by-agent transaction history, and the practical questions buyers and sellers actually ask.

Posts are written by working editors, not by AI generators or paid PR. Where a post is shaped by an interview, the source is named.

## Editorial methodology

Our rankings combine four signals, in roughly equal weight:

- **Verified transaction volume** on the specific lake or sub-market in the trailing 24 months, sourced from Valley MLS, public deed records, and agent-supplied closing data we spot-check.
- **Days-on-market and list-to-sale ratios** for each agent's waterfront listings, normalized to the segment median.
- **Local tenure and specialization** — how long the agent has worked the specific lake, and what share of their book is waterfront.
- **Editorial reference checks** with at least three recent buyer or seller clients per ranked agent.

We do not rank pay-to-play. Agents cannot purchase placement; advertisers are clearly labeled where they appear.

## Advertising disclosure

Smith Lakes Property Review is reader-supported, with limited sponsorships from real-estate-adjacent businesses (title companies, dock builders, lake-area lenders). Sponsorships are visually distinct from editorial. No advertiser receives, or has ever received, a ranking placement or favorable review in exchange for payment.

The editorial desk maintains a strict firewall from sales. Where a sponsor is mentioned in editorial coverage, it is disclosed in-line.

## Corrections policy

If you find a factual error in any post — a transaction number, a license status, a misattributed quote — write to corrections@smithlakespropertyreview.com. We respond within two business days, append a dated correction note to the affected post, and update the underlying ranking when the change is material.
